Why I built this

I used to bookmark good builder quotes from podcasts and never re-read them. Or I'd hear a line I wanted to live by, write it in some note app, then lose it to the abyss of un-tagged documents. The wisdom kept disappearing into noise.

Lenny Rachitsky open-sourced 300+ transcripts of his podcast — interviews with people who built Airbnb, Stripe, Linear, Slack, ChatGPT, Cursor, and a few hundred more. Real operators explaining how they actually decide things. But the format is wrong for how I want to consume it: I don't want a 90-minute audio commitment, I want one sharp line per day.

So I read all 300+ transcripts (well, I had AI agents read them), pulled out the punchiest quotes, and wrote a plain-English unpacking for each one. Now my new tab gives me one of these every morning, plus a Read more if I want the context.

It's not a productivity tool. It's environment design — when my brain is about to default to a habit (open new tab → check something), it gets one good thought first.
